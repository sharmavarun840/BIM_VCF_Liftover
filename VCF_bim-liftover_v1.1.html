All parsing, chain block mapping, and file generation happens in JavaScript in your browser. Your genotype data is never transmitted anywhere. Unmapped variants (falling in gaps, deletions, spanning an alignment block boundary, or landing on unplaced or alternate contigs) are excluded from the lifted output and listed separately, matching standard liftOver behavior. For VCF input, structural variants and breakend records (symbolic ALT alleles like <DEL>, or ALT containing brackets) are not lifted by this tool and are reported as unmapped, since they need dedicated interval logic beyond simple coordinate remapping. When a variant lands on a reverse-strand chain block, REF and ALT are reverse-complemented to stay correct for the new strand, but this tool does not have access to the target build's reference FASTA, so it cannot verify or correct REF against the new assembly the way Picard's LiftoverVcf does with a supplied dictionary. For anything going into a production pipeline, consider a spot-check against the target reference. The downloadable .vcf.gz is standard gzip, readable directly by bcftools, vcftools, and most VCF-aware tools; if you need tabix/bgzip-style indexing, re-compress it with bgzip first. Since a VCF record carries its own genotypes, a lifted VCF is self-contained. Unlike PLINK's .bed/.fam, there's no separate file to keep in sync.
